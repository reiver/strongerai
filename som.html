Self-Organizing Maps

Self-organizing maps appear in a number of places in the brain. The primary visual cortex also called V1 is a famous example of where self-organizing maps are involved in edge-detection. We also see these well organized maps involved with sensory information, with taste maps in the gustatory cortex, in somatotopic maps in the primary somatosensory cortex, etc.

So if our approach to creating strong AI is to create an artificial brain (and let an artificial mind emerge), then understanding how self-organizing maps work can be important.
