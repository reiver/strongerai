Introduction

The original goal of artificial intelligence (AI) was to create human-like intelligence in silico. Modern AI, while useful and instances of (real) intelligence, are (for the most part) nothing like human intelligence.

Given that the AI label has come to cover much more than just human-like intelligence, this goal, of creating human-like intelligence in silico, has been given a new label and is now called strong AI (thus the title of this book), but also goes by the names artificial general intelligence (AGI) and cognitive AI.

(Although some seem to using the label machine learning for non-human-like AI.)
