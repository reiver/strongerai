Stronger AI

by
Charles Iliya Krempeaux

a work in progress

Stronger AI is a book on creating human-like (or for a looser definition, mammal-like) intelligence in silico. I.e., a book on creating strong AI. In addition to the name strong AI, this topic also goes by the names: artificial general intelligence (AGI) and cognitive AI. Only a background in software engineering and an interest in strong AI are necessary to read this book.

You won't become an expert just by reading this book. But, to invoke an idiom: "you have to walk before you can run". This book will help you get started. By reading this book, you will learn the basics and get an idea of what else you need to learn (beyond this book) to advance your knowledge of the topic.

I created this book due to three motivations: #1 that I had the inclination to write it, #2 that there seemed to be no books that introduced someone with only a software engineering background to the topic and #3 to help organize some of my thoughts on the topic. And I suppose #4, because I have a high interest in the topic. Frankly I think there are others more qualified to write this book than I. But as far as I can tell, none of them have written a book like this; so I have. Perhaps someone out there will find it useful or helpful in some way. Although, if the protégé effect is real, then I will have benefitted from writing it.

The format of the book is that there are "normal" chapters that talk about a topic and there are "hands-on" chapters where you "roll-up-your-sleeves" and learn how to implement the things that have been discussed. If you so choose, you can skip the "hands-on" chapters, and only read the "normal" chapters to get an overview of the topic.
